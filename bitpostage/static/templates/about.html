About:

BitPostage was created to fill a void that has been empty in the Bitcoin community. This void was that of shipping and postage paid for with Bitcoins. This issue is a set back to the community in that in order for someone to ship a package or letter to another Bitcoin user they had to exchange for fiat and thus incur exchange fee's and various other banking fee's and long wait times. This is where BitPostage comes in. We provide a service that will turn this exchange of Bitcoins to fiat and make it a simple thirty minute wait. We accept Bitcoins as payment and in turn send you a USPS stamp straight to your email to print out and place on your package or letter. From there you can place it in your mailbox and it will be sent like any other mail. This will make the lives of many people much simpler and we feel make the bitcoin network much stronger.

Fees:

Our fee structure is very simple and is similar to that of the many Bitcoin exchanges, the more you ship the lower your fee will be. It starts at 20% for all new users and will go down each month as long as something is mailed during this month. If you do not wish to register and participate in the fee schedule then you will stay at the 20% fee rate until you register.

Policy

Because of the nature of this service we cannot except returns except for very special and extreme cases. We cannot take responsibility if you enter in the wrong weight, dimensions, or type of postage. We are a as is entity. We email you your stamp and that is the end of the transaction. We provide tracking as a curtiosity to our customers and will remove service to any user that we feel is abusing.

Security

To ensure the security of our customers our code base has to pass a very rigrous testing regime to ensure code quality and to prevent vulnerabilities from propping up. We also have to protect user data in accordance to USPS regulations and Endicia contract regulations. It is in our best interest to provide the best security for our customer's data as possible and we will strive to prevent and minimize our risk of data breach. But, in the event that there is a breach we will take the public site down and notify all customers about the breach along with a break down of the attack, or to the best of our knowledge.

Because of our contracts with the USPS and Endicia we must store the following information about our customers:

  • First and Last name
  • Phone number
  • List of address that they sent post to and when this occured.
  • List of addresses that this customer sent from, ie the return address

We cannot provide this service without this data retention. We need to keep this data in order to protect ourselves and out customers from fraud, crimes, or abuse of our infrastructure and that of the USPS and Endicia. We must follow strict guidelines in handling of this data and will exceed the security recommendations that have been put forth in our contracts with the above listed entities.
